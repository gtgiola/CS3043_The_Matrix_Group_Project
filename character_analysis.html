Ethical Analysis of Main Characters

Neo

  • He is a hacker who sells contraband programming (immoral)
    • Refuses to take responsibility for his actions
      • He warns his customers to never reveal they have had any contact with him
        • “You don’t know me”
  • When he is interrogated by Smith, he is given the choice to either catch a “criminal”, or maintain current status as a criminal himself
    • Yet he decides to continue with his current “criminal” status
  • He decides to engage in more “illicit” activities when he chooses the red pill over the blue one.
  • He risks his life by going back into the Matrix to save Morpheus (moral)
    • This is also an example of his attempts to protect the codes to Zion (information privacy)
  • When he is trying to save Morpheus he engages in immoral behavior:
    • Killing guards
      • Which is actually killing innocent humans to save Morpheus.
      • But at the same time, he believes that saving Morpheus could lead to saving all other humans still trapped in the Matrix.
      • So, he acting towards the greater good.

Morpheus

  • He is trying to save the human race and benefit society. (moral)
    • Acting towards the greater good
  • He sacrifices his own life to save Neo:
    • because he believes Neo is “the One” who can save the human race and benefit society (moral)
  • In his quest to free humans from the Matrix, he recruits rebels by showing them what the real life is outside this computer dream world
    • Is this really a better life? [Cypher])

Agent (Smith)

  • His purpose in “life” is to ensure human oppression (immoral)
  • He uses humans as a means to an end. (immoral)
    • As a source of energy
  • He uses people as hosts whenever he wants, thus terminating that person’s existence to ensure his (immoral)

Cypher

  • He betrays his colleagues to satisfy his desire to return to the Matrix (immoral)
    • Selfish
    • Killing people in the process

Trinity

  • Famous hacker who had previously cracked the IRS D-base system
  • Selflessness: she demands to go back into the Matrix with Neo to save Morpheus) (moral)
    • Acting towards the greater good
  • When she is trying to save Morpheus she engages in immoral behavior:
    • Killing guards
      • Which is actually killing innocent humans to save Morpheus.
      • But at the same time, she believes that saving Morpheus could lead to saving all other humans still trapped in the Matrix.
      • So, she acting towards the greater good.

Tank

  • He is responsible for providing the resources (weapons, helicopter lessons, etc.) for the rebels, thus facilitating their cyber attacks into the Matrix
  • He kills Cypher after his betrayal
    • He is killing a criminal
    • He trying to save other people’s lives
    • He seems to be acting towards the greater good
    • Yet, is it still ok to take someone else’s life no matter how evil that person is?
